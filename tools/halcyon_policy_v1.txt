HALCYON CREDIT — LENDING POLICY KNOWLEDGE BASE
Version 1.0 | Effective Date: January 2025
Classification: Internal — Underwriting Operations

This document defines the complete set of lending policy clauses used by Halcyon Credit
for consumer loan underwriting decisions. All automated and human underwriting decisions
must be consistent with the clauses defined herein. Each clause is assigned a unique
identifier and hard-stop status. Hard-stop clauses mandate an automatic decline and
cannot be overridden by discretion alone.

================================================================================
CLAUSE POL-001 — Debt-to-Income Ceiling (HARD STOP)
================================================================================
Policy ID    : POL-001
Category     : Affordability
Hard Stop    : YES
Threshold    : DTI > 40%
Applies To   : All applicant types (salaried, self-employed, gig, commercial)

Full Text:
Halcyon Credit's maximum allowable debt-to-income ratio for all standard consumer
loan applicants is forty percent (40%). Debt-to-income ratio is calculated as the
sum of all existing monthly debt obligations divided by the applicant's gross monthly
income, expressed as a percentage. A DTI above 40% indicates that existing obligations
already consume a disproportionate share of the applicant's income before the new loan
payment is added, creating an unacceptable repayment risk. Applications where the
verified DTI exceeds 40% must be declined regardless of credit score, income level,
loan purpose, or other mitigating factors. This clause is a hard stop and is not
subject to senior underwriter override without formal credit committee review.

Rationale: Empirical data from the LendingClub loan portfolio shows default rates
climbing steeply above DTI=35%, reaching 38% at DTI=50%. The 40% ceiling balances
access to credit with responsible lending.

CLAUSE POL-002 — Public Record Hard Stop (HARD STOP)
================================================================================
Policy ID    : POL-002
Category     : Credit History
Hard Stop    : YES
Threshold    : Any public record present (pub_rec >= 1)
Applies To   : All applicant types

Full Text:
Any applicant with one or more public records on file — including but not limited to
bankruptcies, civil judgements, tax liens, or wage garnishments — must be declined.
Public records represent legal findings of financial failure or fraud and are the
strongest individual predictors of future default available in the credit bureau data.
This is an unconditional hard stop. No volume of positive compensating factors
(high income, low DTI, strong credit score) can override a positive public record
finding. Applicants with a public record who believe the record is in error must
dispute it with the relevant credit bureau before reapplying.

Rationale: A public record (particularly bankruptcy) increases default probability
by a factor of 3-5x relative to otherwise similar applicants. Halcyon Credit cannot
price this risk at retail consumer loan rates.

CLAUSE POL-003 — High Loan-to-Income Ratio Review (ADVISORY FLAG)
================================================================================
Policy ID    : POL-003
Category     : Affordability
Hard Stop    : NO
Threshold    : loan_amount / annual_income > 3.0
Applies To   : All applicant types

Full Text:
When the requested loan amount exceeds three times (3.0x) the applicant's verified
annual income, the application must be flagged for senior underwriter review before
a final decision is issued. This threshold signals that the loan represents a
significant multiple of the applicant's earning capacity, increasing vulnerability
to income disruption. Automated approval is not permitted when this flag is triggered.
The reviewing underwriter must document the compensating factors that justify approval
if a positive decision is made. This clause does not mandate decline — a well-qualified
applicant with a loan-to-income ratio above 3.0 may still be approved with appropriate
conditions.

CLAUSE POL-004 — Debt Consolidation Income Re-verification (ADVISORY FLAG)
================================================================================
Policy ID    : POL-004
Category     : Loan Purpose
Hard Stop    : NO
Threshold    : loan_purpose = debt_consolidation AND DTI > 35%
Applies To   : All applicant types

Full Text:
Debt consolidation applications submitted by applicants with a debt-to-income ratio
above thirty-five percent (35%) require mandatory income re-verification before
approval. Borrowers seeking to consolidate existing debt at elevated DTI levels are
frequently in active financial distress at the time of application. Stated income
figures for this segment are more likely to be overstated or based on variable
income sources. Re-verification must confirm the income figure through at least one
of: employer payroll confirmation, most recent two months of bank statements, or
prior year tax return. This is an advisory flag, not a hard stop — the application
may proceed to approval once re-verification is complete.

CLAUSE POL-005 — Thin File Referral Mandate (ADVISORY FLAG)
================================================================================
Policy ID    : POL-005
Category     : Credit History — Thin File
Hard Stop    : NO — automatic decline is PROHIBITED
Threshold    : credit_age_months < 24 OR open_accounts < 3
Applies To   : All applicant types

Full Text:
Applicants who qualify as thin-file borrowers — defined as those with a credit history
shorter than twenty-four (24) months OR fewer than three (3) open credit accounts —
must be referred to a human underwriter for review. Automatic decline of thin-file
applicants is explicitly prohibited under Halcyon Credit's fair lending commitment
and is not supported by empirical evidence. Analysis of the Home Credit dataset shows
that thin-file applicants, when evaluated holistically including income stability,
employment duration, and purpose, default at only 2-3 percentage points above
applicants with established credit histories. Human review must assess non-bureau
signals including: length of employment, income verification confidence, payment
history on utilities, and stated loan purpose. The reviewing underwriter may approve,
refer, or decline after documenting the specific factors considered.

Rationale: Thin-file applicants represent the core underserved segment Halcyon
Credit exists to serve. An automatic decline policy for this group would undermine
the company's mission and expose Halcyon to fair lending regulatory risk.

CLAUSE POL-006 — Multiple Delinquencies Referral (ADVISORY FLAG)
================================================================================
Policy ID    : POL-006
Category     : Credit History
Hard Stop    : NO
Threshold    : delinquencies in past 24 months >= 2
Applies To   : All applicant types

Full Text:
Applicants with two or more delinquencies (30+ days past due) recorded in the past
twenty-four months must be referred for senior underwriter review. A single delinquency
may reflect a one-time hardship event; two or more delinquencies within a 24-month
window suggest a pattern of payment difficulty that requires human judgement to
properly contextualize. The reviewing underwriter must assess whether the delinquencies
are recent or historic, whether they cluster around a documented hardship event
(job loss, medical emergency), and whether the applicant's current financial position
has materially improved. Approval is permitted with documented compensating factors.

CLAUSE POL-007 — Minimum Credit Score Hard Stop (HARD STOP)
================================================================================
Policy ID    : POL-007
Category     : Credit Quality
Hard Stop    : YES
Threshold    : credit_score < 580 AND thin_file = FALSE
Applies To   : Standard (thick-file) applicants only

Full Text:
Standard applicants — those who do not qualify as thin-file under POL-005 — with a
credit score below five hundred and eighty (580) must be declined. A credit score
below 580 in an applicant with an established credit history (24+ months, 3+ accounts)
reflects a documented pattern of credit mismanagement rather than simple lack of
history. At this score level, default probability significantly exceeds Halcyon
Credit's risk tolerance at retail consumer loan pricing. This hard stop explicitly
does NOT apply to thin-file applicants, who are handled under POL-005 and must
always be referred rather than automatically declined.

Note: For applicants whose credit score is derived from an external scoring model
(such as Home Credit's EXT_SOURCE_2 normalised to the 300-850 range), the same
threshold of 580 applies. The normalisation methodology is documented in the
dataset card.
